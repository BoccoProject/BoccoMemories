We were getting to know each other now, learning the important facts: you had a pink flip phone and a very long last name, I wore decade-old jeans and was very good at eating Cheerios.

We were getting to be known by someone else, too—finishing up our drinks in a bar overlooking the road and walking back in from the balcony, we saw a group of waiters staring at a bank of camera monitors showing our table from multiple angles.

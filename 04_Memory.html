A porch in a storm

We were effectively living together in a flat in Dalston hidden behind some tacky store or other and listening to foxes enjoy (?) death-scream-intimacy every night.

Returning one day along the walkway linking an upper storey of the building's units, we stopped on the porch outside our door. I lit a cigarette and it began to rain.

You laid down beyond the edge of the porch, getting soaking wet. I leaned over and kissed you, and you said—that you were waiting for the lightning.

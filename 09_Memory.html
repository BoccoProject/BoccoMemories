An island for fishing

I don't know that I've ever been exhausted as I was when we jumped onto the train in Florence seconds before departure. I tasted of salt the whole long way up Italy to a transfer in Milan and on to the Piedmontese countryside.

I don't know that I've ever been as confused as I believe both of us to have been when we got to Stresa and had to figure out how to travel onwards, onto the lake. It was another close call, catching the last boat of the day out to the island.

That night we sat on a dock floating above Lago Maggiore and watched the immense, magnified moon rise. The now-dead day lingered in our silver shadows on the water.
